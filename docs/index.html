COMBAT CHESS
Capture · Challenge · Fight

It’s chess. With fists.

Combat Chess is real, full-rules chess — until a capture is contested. Then both pieces drop into a real-time retro pixel brawl, and the winner keeps the square. No piece goes quietly. Not even the king.

Action Cards: fight for the square

You start every match with 3 Action Cards. When your piece is captured, spend one to force a fight instead of just losing it. Win the brawl and the capture is repelled — your piece holds the square and the attacker is removed. Cards are spent win or lose and never refill mid-match. Run dry, and captures land like ordinary chess.

Damage persists for the entire match. Wound the queen now, hunt her down later — a health bar follows every piece that has fought. A pawn can topple a queen. It just takes near-flawless play.

Six-button arcade combat

  • Left punch (fast jab) and right punch (slow, heavy cross).
  • Side-matched blocking — read the tell and guard the correct side to absorb 80% of the hit.
  • Timed dodges — perfect timing negates all damage and charges your meter.
  • Stamina that punishes button-mashing. Burn out and you’re exhausted: unable to act and taking 1.5× damage.
  • Land perfect dodges to unlock the SUPER — a single power shot worth 2.5× a heavy punch.

Spam loses. Reads win.

The king never falls quietly

Checkmate isn’t automatically the end — it triggers a mandatory final fight: your king duels the mating piece at 300 HP, no card needed. Win the last stand and the mate is broken. This cuts both ways: to beat you, your opponent must finish the job in the ring.

Frequently asked questions

How do Action Cards work?

Each player starts every match with 3 Action Cards. When one of your pieces is captured, you can spend a card to force a fight between your piece and the attacker instead of just losing it. Win the fight and your piece keeps the square while the attacker is removed. Cards are spent whether you win or lose, and they never refill during a match. Once you’re out, captures resolve like normal chess.

Why didn’t checkmate end my game?

By design! In Combat Chess, checkmate triggers a mandatory final fight: the mated king duels the mating piece at 300 HP, no card needed. If the king wins the last stand, the mate is broken and play continues. To win by checkmate, you have to finish the enemy king in the ring — and the same applies when you’re the one who’s mated. While merely in check, you can also choose to spend a card and send your king to fight the checking piece directly instead of moving.

How do fights online work? Am I fighting my friend live?

Not live — online matches are turn-based, so you’re rarely both holding your phones at the same moment. When you contest a capture, the fight resolves immediately on your own device against a computer-controlled version of your opponent’s piece. The CPU proxy uses the real piece’s stats: its true HP (including any damage it carries from earlier fights) and its true punching power. The result is final and binding, and your opponent sees a full recap of what happened on their next turn. Live head-to-head fights are on the roadmap for a future update.

What is the 4-day forfeit rule?

If you don’t make a move in an online match within 4 days, you forfeit and your opponent wins by default. While the clock is running you’ll get one reminder per day (“3 / 2 / 1 day(s) left”). Reminders are scheduled on your device, so open the app at least once after your opponent moves to make sure they’re set.

Why does online play need Game Center?

Combat Chess has no server of its own. All matchmaking, turn delivery, and move notifications are handled by Apple’s Game Center, which is built into iOS. Sign in under Settings → Game Center on your iPhone, then invite a friend from the game’s online menu. Single-player never requires Game Center or an internet connection.

Does the game work offline?

Yes — single-player against the computer works fully offline, including fights, match history, and settings. Only online multiplayer needs a connection (and Game Center).

How do I change the difficulty or CPU strength?

Open Settings in the app. Each difficulty tier maps to a Stockfish rating you can tune with a slider, from a friendly 600 Elo up to a superhuman 3190. Below 1320 Elo the engine plays with intentionally limited skill; above it, strength is Elo-calibrated. Sound effects and haptics can be toggled in the same place.

How do I clear my play history?

Open History in the app. Swipe left on any match to delete it individually, or use Clear Play History at the bottom to permanently delete every match and fight record on your device. In-progress games are not affected. History is stored only on your device, so deleting the app also deletes it.

I’m not getting notifications when my opponent moves.

Check that notifications are allowed under Settings → Notifications → Combat Chess, and that you’re signed in under Settings → Game Center. Game Center delivers the “your move” push itself; the daily forfeit-countdown reminders are scheduled locally, so they require you to have opened the app at least once after the opponent’s move.

Why does a health bar follow some of my pieces?

Any piece that has fought carries its damage for the rest of the match — the health bar shows how hurt it is. A wounded piece enters its next fight at that reduced HP. Health resets between matches.

Is the game really free and open source?

Yes. The complete source code is public at github.com/PunchManDev/CombatChess under the GNU GPL v3 — see the Legal tab. There are no ads, no in-app purchases required to play, no tracking, and no accounts.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026

Short version: Combat Chess does not collect, store, or share any personal data. There is no analytics, no advertising, no tracking, and no account with us. There is no server of ours for your data to be sent to.

What the app stores — on your device only

  • Game data. Your saved single-player game, match history (wins/losses, fight statistics), and settings (difficulty, CPU Elo, sound, haptics) are stored locally on your device. They never leave it, except as described under Game Center below. Deleting the app deletes this data.
  • Notifications. If you allow notifications, the app schedules reminders locally on your device for online matches awaiting your move. Nothing about these is sent anywhere.

Game Center (optional — only if you play online)

Online multiplayer is provided by Apple’s Game Center. If you choose to play online, Apple handles the matchmaking, your Game Center identity, and the delivery of turns and notifications. In that case:

  • Your Game Center nickname and your game moves are transmitted through Apple’s servers to your opponent, so that you can play. That is inherent to playing an online match.
  • The developer of Combat Chess operates no server and receives, stores, and has access to none of this data.
  • Apple’s handling of Game Center data is governed by Apple’s own privacy policy: apple.com/legal/privacy

If you never use online play, no data leaves your device at all.

What we do NOT do

  • We do not collect personal information.
  • We do not use analytics or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • We do not show ads.
  • We do not track you across apps or websites.
  • We do not sell or share data with third parties (we have none to sell).

Children

Combat Chess is safe for general audiences and collects no data from anyone, including children.
